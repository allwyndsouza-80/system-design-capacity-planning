Who uses your app?
How many people use your app every day? iCalled "DAU" (Daily Active Users). Example: 1,000,000 means one million people open your app today. Every other calculation starts from this number.

How many times does one user tap or click per day? iEach tap = one request to your server. Example: user opens app, scrolls feed 5 times, posts a photo, likes 3 things = about 10 actions. This gets multiplied by all users to find your total daily server load.
How much busier does it get at rush hour? iTraffic is never even all day. At lunch or evenings your app may get 5x more traffic than the average. This multiplier helps you plan for the worst-case spike. Most apps use 3–5x. A live event or ticketing app might need 10–20x.

What % of requests are users loading something? iA "read" = loading or viewing data. Scrolling a feed, searching, opening a profile page — all reads. They hit your database or cache to fetch data. Most apps are 70–90% reads.
Loading a page, viewing a profile, searching = reading data

What % of requests are users saving something? iA "write" = creating or changing data. Posting, liking, buying, commenting — all writes. Note: these can overlap with reads! Viewing a post can also write a view count. That is why both sliders are independent here.
Posting, liking, buying, commenting = writing data

How big is one request or response? (KB) iEvery server response has a size. A small JSON reply is 1–5 KB. A feed page with images can be 50–200 KB. This is used to calculate how much storage space you will fill up over time.
Average data size per request in kilobytes — e.g. 10 KB
How many days should data be kept? iHow long before old data is deleted or archived. 365 = keep one year of data. The longer you keep it, the more storage you need. This directly multiplies your daily storage growth.
365 = keep one year of data before archiving
